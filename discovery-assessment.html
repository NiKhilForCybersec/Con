Discovery & Assessment

The foundation of every successful SIEM migration is understanding what you're migrating from. Discovery isn't just an inventory exerciseâ€”it's risk identification, effort estimation, and success criteria definition.

Why Discovery Matters

I've seen migrations fail because teams skipped proper discovery. They assumed they knew their environment, underestimated content complexity, and ended up with a 6-month project that took 18 months.

Discovery answers critical questions:

  • What data are we collecting? Sources, volumes, collection methods
  • What content exists? Rules, dashboards, reports, lookups
  • What's actually being used? Active vs dormant content
  • Who depends on this? Users, teams, integrations
  • What are the risks? Gaps, dependencies, blockers

Extracting Volume Metrics

Use your existing SIEM to quantify data sources:

Splunk: Daily Volume by Sourcetype
index=* earliest=-30d latest=now
| eval GB=round(bytes/1024/1024/1024, 2)
| stats sum(GB) as total_GB by sourcetype
| sort -total_GB
| eval daily_avg_GB = round(total_GB/30, 2)
| table sourcetype, daily_avg_GB, total_GB
Sentinel: Daily Volume by Table
Usage
| where TimeGenerated > ago(30d)
| where IsBillable == true
| summarize TotalGB = sum(Quantity) / 1000 by DataType
| extend DailyAvgGB = round(TotalGB / 30, 2)
| order by TotalGB desc
| project DataType, DailyAvgGB, TotalGB

Content Inventory

Detection rules, dashboards, and reports represent significant investment. Understanding what existsâ€”and what's actually usedâ€”determines migration effort.

Content Categories

Content Type Migration Effort Key Considerations
Detection Rules High (2-8 hrs each) Query translation, schema mapping, threshold tuning
Dashboards Medium (1-4 hrs each) Visualization recreation, data availability
Scheduled Reports Medium (1-2 hrs each) Query translation, scheduling, distribution
Lookup Tables Low-Medium Data migration, format conversion
Macros/Functions Medium Often embedded in rules - identify dependencies
Response Playbooks High Complete rebuild in new SOAR platform

The Active vs Dormant Challenge

Most organizations have significant "dark matter" in their SIEMâ€”content that exists but provides no value:

  • Rules that never fire - Detection logic doesn't match current data
  • Rules that always fire - So noisy they're auto-closed or ignored
  • Disabled rules - Turned off months ago, never re-enabled
  • Orphan dashboards - Created for a project, never used since
âš ï¸ The 80/20 Reality

In my experience, 80% of detection value comes from 20% of rules. During discovery, I identify that critical 20% for priority migration. The rest gets evaluatedâ€”many won't be migrated at all.

Risk Identification

Discovery should surface risks that could derail migration:

Risk Category Example Risks Mitigation
Data Custom parsing that's undocumented; sources with no native connector Document all parsing; identify custom connector requirements early
Content Complex correlation rules; heavy macro usage Budget extra translation time; plan for rewrites
People Key person dependency; team unfamiliar with target platform Knowledge transfer; early training investment
Process Change management bottlenecks; audit requirements Engage change board early; plan for audit continuity
Technical Network restrictions; authentication complexity Validate connectivity; plan auth integration

Discovery Deliverables

At the end of discovery, I produce:

  1. Data Source Inventory - Complete catalog with volumes and migration approach
  2. Content Assessment - Rules, dashboards, reports with priority rating
  3. Integration Map - All connections with migration path
  4. Risk Register - Identified risks with mitigation plans
  5. Effort Estimate - Realistic timeline and resource requirements
  6. Migration Waves - Proposed phasing based on dependencies and priority
